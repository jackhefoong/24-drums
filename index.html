History

Founders

Founded 24-Festival Drums in 1988.

In April 1988, Malaysia Chinese Association joint the five main Association in Johor Bahru such as ChaoZhou Association, Hakka Association, FuJian Association, Guangdong Association and Hainan Association and funded the first twenty-four Festival Drums support team established in Foon Yew High School. It became a national heritage project of Malaysia in 2009.

24 Festival Drums is a drum art performance combining 24 seasons, calligraphy and Cantonese lion drums. Performers create different drum beat rhythms for each seasonal drum, with shouts, body movements and changes in formation to form a large drum performance. The Twenty-Four Season Drums originated from the National Chinese Dance Festival organized by the Chinese Association of Johor Bahru in April 1988. The theme of the festival "Nine Dances" represents the 9th National Chinese Dance Festival. The committee planned Tan Chai Puan's original idea to use nine southern lion drums at the opening ceremony to convey the lively atmosphere of the festival with the thrill of drums. His idea was given to the music creation "Nine Drums of Thunder" provided by composer Tan Hooi Song. Mr.Tan Chai Puan felt that the twenty-four solar terms of the handwritten modern poems were beautiful in calligraphy, 12 Singaporean and Malaysian calligraphers engraved the 24 seasons on the sides of the drums in the ancient temple of Johor on June 12, 1988. Malaysia’s first Twenty-Four Seasonal Drum Team was established at Johor Bahru Kuan Rou Middle School in April 1988. The art gradually spread to elementary schools, middle schools, colleges, associations, halls and temples across the country, and to overseas countries such as Singapore , China, Taiwan and many more.

Mr Tan Chai Puan

A cultural activist, businessman, poet and cartoonist.

Mr Tan Hooi Song

A renowned musician who was bestowed the Warisan Orang Hidup award by the ministry for his role in creating the 24-Festival Drums.

24 Seasons

A typical Malaysian 24 Seasons unit consists of twenty four drums. The number twenty four is not an arbitrary selection. It represents agricultural seasons from the Chinese calendar. If you look closely you will find the name of each season painted in calligraphy on each drum.
Even though each drum troupe has its own individual style, the original styles of performance were meant to depict movements of farmers and activities on a farm.

HANDS Percussion

Founder

Founded HANDS Percussion in 1997

With a small team of full-time performers all of whom were well under way to becoming brilliant musicians, HANDS began a musical journey of a lifetime. Despite the challenges of being a self-funded group they are now a crowd-favorite at local performing arts venues, with a steadily growing reputation locally and internationally. They are Malaysia's first professional 24 Festive Drum Team. Their performances in recent years combine traditional percussion instruments such as gamelan, Malay drums, and Indian drums to represent the diverse cultures of Malaysia Colors.

The NEXT 2014

Having rocked the stage in November 2012 with their performance of THE NEXT, HANDS proved that they are on the right path of ‘Trending Percussive Music’ by winning awards for Best Original Composition, Best Musical Director, Best Solo Performance (Instrumental), Best Group Performance (Instrumental) and Best Production Values at the 10th BOH Cameronian Arts Awards 2013. Wind of Nomads

August 2017

Since the beginning of time, humans have migrated, reimagining and rebuilding their lives throughout beauty, tolerance and innovation. Wind of Nomads is a metaphor reflecting human and cultural migration, the passage of energies throughout generations, globalization and integration. This work is inspired by the soul of two of the world’s great deserts in Africa and China.

Armour and Skin

July 2019

The gamelan is made of metal, yet speaks gently and elegantly. The drums are fashioned from supple skin, yet is famous for its loud outbursts! Like armour and skin, these two instruments move over the same contours. Both echo, and in both instances, accurate damping of the instrument is crucial for desired effect. Instruments and artists awaken, embracing the spirit of the arts, the natural inner essence of an individual. The Balinese believe that it is this spirit (an ancient Balinese concept), along with a little divine inspiration that motivates an artist to great heights. Armour and Skin is a celebration of our inner spirits, instruments and music and the magic that happens when everything, even discord seem to fall in place for a single purpose - hope.
